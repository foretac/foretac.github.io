ForeTac: Steering Robot Actions with Predicted Contact Consequences

Predictive tactile guidance for contact-rich robot manipulation

Anonymous Authors

Anonymous Institution

Paper arXiv Code (coming soon) Videos

Abstract

Contact-rich manipulation demands policies that anticipate contact consequences before committing to actions. We present ForeTac, a framework that steers robot actions with predicted contact consequences. ForeTac learns an action-conditioned tactile foresight model that predicts future contact states from candidate action chunks, and a contact quality energy model that scores predicted outcomes for physical plausibility.

At inference time, the energy model provides gradient signals for bounded trust-region refinement of the generated action chunk—correcting it toward contact-safe trajectories without retraining the base policy. Additionally, the same foresight model can regularize policy training by penalizing actions that produce unfavorable predicted contact.

We evaluate ForeTac on real-robot contact-rich tasks including board wiping and peg insertion, comparing against visual diffusion policies, reactive tactile fusion, candidate reranking, and foresight-only baselines. ForeTac reduces contact failures while maintaining task success, demonstrating that predicted tactile consequences are an effective and efficient action guidance signal.

Key Idea

Predict

Action-conditioned tactile foresight model predicts future contact states from candidate action chunks.

Score

Contact quality energy model evaluates predicted tactile outcomes—higher score means safer, more effective contact.

Guide

Energy gradient drives bounded trust-region refinement of the action chunk at inference time, without retraining the policy.

Method Overview

ForeTac is a three-stage framework: predict future tactile consequences, score them with a learned energy model, and guide the action via bounded gradient ascent.

Figure 1. ForeTac architecture. The base Diffusion Policy generates a clean action chunk. The Foresight Transformer predicts its future tactile consequences. The Contact Quality Energy Model scores these predictions. Trust-region gradient ascent refines the action toward higher contact quality.

Training Pipeline

Figure 2. Four-stage training pipeline. Each stage builds on frozen modules from previous stages. TacVAE provides tactile representations; Foresight learns to predict future latents; DP generates actions; Energy Scorer learns contact quality assessment.

Inference-Time Guidance

Figure 3. Trust-region guided refinement. The energy score gradient with respect to the action provides a direction of improvement. Each step is projected back into a bounded trust region around the original action, ensuring stability.
